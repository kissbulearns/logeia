Logeia Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

The short version: Logeia collects nothing. Everything you save stays on your device. There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics, no ads, and no third-party SDKs.

What we collect

Nothing. Logeia has no backend and no analytics. We never see your saved items, your searches, your questions, or anything else. The app contains no third-party tracking or advertising code.

Where your data lives

Everything you save — articles, posts, transcripts, notes, summaries — is stored in a database on your iPhone, protected by iOS Data Protection (encrypted at rest). AI summaries and answers are generated on your device by Apple Intelligence; your content is not sent to any cloud AI service by Logeia.

Network access

Logeia connects to the internet for exactly one purpose: fetching the content you ask it to save (the article page, post, or video transcript and thumbnail). These requests go directly from your device to the source website. No request is routed through, or logged by, any server of ours — none exists.

Backup — and what happens if you delete the app

Today, your library exists only on your device (and inside your own device backups, if you have iPhone backups enabled). If you uninstall Logeia without a device backup, your saved library may be permanently lost.

An optional backup feature is planned that syncs your library to your own iCloud account (Apple's CloudKit private database). When it ships: it will be off by default, the data will live in your iCloud — readable by you, governed by Apple's iCloud terms — and we still will not be able to access it.

Your content, your rights

Everything in Logeia is yours. Delete any item, or the whole app, at any time — deletion is immediate and local. There is no account to close and no data of yours anywhere for us to delete.

Children's privacy

Logeia does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices ever change (for example, when optional iCloud backup ships), this policy will be updated here before the change takes effect, with the date above revised.

Contact

Questions about privacy or anything else: open an issue on GitHub Issues.
